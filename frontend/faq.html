Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about reporting road issues and how our system works.

How do I report a new road issue?

Simply navigate to the "Report Issue" page, fill in the title, category, and description. Most importantly, pin the location on the map and upload a clear photo of the issue. Once you submit, it will appear on our public dashboard.

How long does it take for an issue to be fixed?

Fix times vary depending on the severity of the issue and budget allocation. Potholes are usually prioritized for safety. You can track the status of any report on the Dashboard—it will change from "Reported" to "In Progress" and finally "Resolved".

Can I report an issue without a photo?

Yes, you can. However, reports with high-quality photos are significantly more helpful for repair crews to assess the equipment and materials needed before they arrive on site.

Is my personal information public?

No. Currently, RoadFix does not require individual user accounts or display person-identifiable information on reports. Only the issue details and location are public.

Who actually fixes the roads?

RoadFix is a communication platform. The actual repairs are carried out by the local Public Works Department or contracted maintenance crews after receiving your data through our system.
